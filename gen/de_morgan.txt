De Morgan's Laws:
For any propositions A and B:
1. Negation of a disjunction: \neg (A \lor B) = (\neg A) \land (\neg B)
2. Negation of a conjunction: \neg (A \land B) = (\neg A) \lor (\neg B)

Set theory application:
For sets A and B:
1. \overline{A \cap B} = \overline{A} \cup \overline{B}
2. \overline{A \cup B} = \overline{A} \cap \overline{B}

Generalizing De Morgan Duality:
Universal quantifier: \forall x P(x) \equiv \neg [\exists x (\neg P(x))]
Existential quantifier: \exists x P(x) \equiv \neg [\forall x (\neg P(x))]

Extension to Modal Logic:
Necessity and possibility operators:
1. \Box p \equiv \neg \Diamond \neg p
2. \Diamond p \equiv \neg \Box \neg p

Application in Computer Engineering:
De Morgan's laws are extensively used for simplifying circuit designs.
